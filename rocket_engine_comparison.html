🧠 Fast Takeaway

Raptor 3 is the thrust king in this set.

RS-25 is the efficiency king, thanks to liquid hydrogen and a vacuum specific impulse around 452 seconds.

Merlin 1D is the high-cadence workhorse: smaller, simpler, and extremely proven.

RD-181 is a kerosene bruiser: very high chamber pressure, strong sea-level thrust, and excellent efficiency for LOX/RP-1.

📚 Sources and Notes

  • RS-25 / SSME: L3Harris RS-25 page lists LOX/LH2 propellants, staged-combustion cycle, 418,000 lbf sea-level thrust, 512,300 lbf vacuum thrust, 366 s sea-level Isp, 452 s vacuum Isp, 2,994 psia chamber pressure, and 7,775 lb weight.
  • Raptor 1–3: SpaceX 2024 Raptor 3 comparison values are widely reproduced: Raptor 1 at 185 tf, 2,080 kg, 250 bar, 350 s; Raptor 2 at 230 tf, 1,630 kg, 300 bar, 347 s; Raptor 3 at 280 tf, 1,525 kg, 330 bar, 350 s.
  • RD-181 / RD-191 family: public RD-191/RD-181-family specifications list about 196 tf sea-level thrust, 212 tf vacuum thrust, about 311 s sea-level Isp, about 338–339 s vacuum Isp, 2,200 kg dry mass, and about 262.6 kgf/cm² chamber pressure.
  • Merlin 1D: public Merlin 1D/Falcon 9 figures vary by revision. This table uses the commonly cited current sea-level Merlin range around 190,000 lbf and Merlin Vacuum thrust around 220,500 lbf.
